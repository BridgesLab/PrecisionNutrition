The primary result, using the inverse variance weighted method shows a 0.064511 \(\pm\) 0.0187438 SD increase in calcium (MGI-BioVU LabWAS) per 1 SD increase in total cholesterol (UK Biobank). This is statistically significant with a p-value of 5.7800887^{-4}. Three of the four MR methods (IVW, weighted median, weighted mode) gave consistent, significant causal estimates, supporting the hypothesis that total cholesterol may impact serum calcium levels in a positive direction. The MR-Egger estimate was not statistically significant, but this method is known to have low power.
